Research Interests

Unveiling Surprising Effects of Bacteriostatic Drugs

I'm really curious about how bacteria react to drugs that slow them down (bacteriostatic drugs). These drugs not only slow their growth but also make them change shape and sometimes even burst! Studying this helps us figure out how to mix these drugs with others to make them work better and burst bacteria.

Mystery of Microbial Slumber

Just like bears preparing for hibernation, bacteria enter a dormant state when food is scarce, awaiting the next feast. But what's happening inside as they transition from "party mode" to "sleep mode"? What preparations are needed for the next feast?

Heterogeneity in Clones

Even in a group of identical cells, everyone is different. One cell can survive antibiotics better than others. Or one cell wakes up earlier than others. Why? Small differences between cells can lead to huge outcomes. After all, the survivor is the one that takes over the world!

Agar Art

Did you know that bacteria can be used to create amazing art? In clinical microbiology, we use special agar plates to identify bacteria by their reactions. Some bacteria even produce natural pigments, allowing us to create a vibrant color palette. The beauty of agar art lies in its fleeting nature—time and incubation conditions can completely transform the outcome, making each piece a unique, short-lived masterpiece.
