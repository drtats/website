Day 1 (Monday): Upper Body - Chest Focus

- Always good to start with warm up sets to check the form and condition. Typically, 50% weight 8-10 reps, 80% 8-10 reps, 100% 2-3 reps with 1min interval. - 8-10 or 10-12 rep range is good. It can be 12-15 or more for some exercise but you do not want to reach mental failure before physical failure. Working set means you lift till failure so do not stop if you exceed rep range. Increase weight as you exceed rep range.

Routines

1. Dumbbell Press (Range)

Dumbbell press is safer and great for inner muscle training although you cannot go heavy. Lock your back before lift and keep locked to avoid shoulder injury. Focus on range of motion and stretch at bottom.
Dumbbell press and dumbbell rows can be combined as a superset to cut time.

2. Rows (Horizontal)

Select one:
1. Barbel rows (Smith or Free)
2. Dumbell rows
3. Incline Bench Dumbbell rows
Range of motion is very important for rows, especially negative. Lock your shoulders like when you do bench press to reduce the shoulder injury while stretching at bottom. Contract back to lift, slowly go down, feel the back stretch.
#1 allows you to lift heavy but you might have hard time keeping a good form (or damage your lower back...). #3 is recommended if you have an adjustable bench. Set bench to 45 degree or lower and lay on it. Bench gives you the support so you can focus on using back muscles.

3. Press (Strength)

Select one:
1. Barbel press (Smith or Free)
2. Press machine
Press machine is safer and you can go heavy without having a mental stop. You can go heavier with barbel bench press but you might have mental stop. Smith machine is great for safety. Free weight allows you to train inner muscles to balance.Make sure to lock your back to reduce injury. Lift your hip to go even heavier (not necessary).
